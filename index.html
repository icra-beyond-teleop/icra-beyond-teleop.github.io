ICRA workshop on

Beyond Teloperation: Learning from Diverse Human and Simulation Data

Introduction

Leveraging large-scale human data holds great potential for scaling up robot learning in unstructured, human-centered environments. While collecting teleoperated robot demonstrations is slow and expensive, human demonstrations are abundant, easy to capture, and richly embedded in human-centric environments. With the recent advances in 3D vision, generative modeling, and large-scale policy training, we are getting closer to overcoming the major challenges of using human data for scalable robot learning.

This workshop asks the pivotal question: How can we effectively translate this wealth of passive human data into robust robotic action?

The Beyond Teloperation: Learning from Diverse Human and Simulation Data workshop intends to answer this question in the following aspects:

  • Perception: How can we build robust and accurate systems for 3D human and object tracking, contact estimation, and affordance recognition from video?
  • Representation Learning: What are the right state and action representations for bridging the human-robot embodiment gap?
  • World Models: How can we leverage video data to learn predictive models of the world that can be used for planning, control, and simulation?
  • Learning in the Loop: How can we incorporate human feedback and active learning to improve the efficiency and robustness of the learning process?
  • Collaborative Manipulation: How can we extend these techniques to scenarios involving human-robot collaboration and multi-agent interaction?

This workshop features invited talks, contributed spotlights, posters, and panel discussions to bring together researchers in the fields of robotics, artificial intelligence, and computer vision and foster discussions on how to solve general-purpose robot manipulation via learning from humans.

Submission Guidelines

  • Page Limit: There are no page length requirements, but we suggest a length around 4-9 pages long. There is no limit on the number of pages for references or appendices.
  • Formatting: Submissions are encouraged to use the RSS 2025, IROS 2025, CoRL 2025, ICRA 2026, ICLR 2026 templates.
  • Anonymity: All submissions must be anonymized. Please remove any author names, affiliations, or identifying information.
  • Relevant Work: We welcome references to recently published, relevant work.
  • Archival Status: All accepted papers are non-archival. Submitting to multiple non-archival workshops is permitted.
  • Submission Link: OpenReview Submission
  • Presentation: Accepted papers will be presented in the form of posters at the workshop. In addition, selected papers may be invited to deliver spotlight talks.

Paper topics

A non-exhaustive list of relevant topics:

  • Perception Methods for Parsing and Processing Human Videos
  • Representation Learning from Human Videos for Downstream Robotics Applications
  • Data Collection Hardware and Systems for Learning from Humans
  • Retargeting Methods for Learning Robot Policies from Humans
  • Learning World Models from In-the-wild Videos
  • Real-to-sim-to-real Methods for Learning from Human Videos
  • Human-in-the-loop Methods for Improving Robot Policies
  • Methods for Collaborative Manipulation
